## Tax Burden Comparison

**The problem**: Income tax is deducted before you receive your money. The velocity mechanism applies only to idle surplus after all expenses are met.

| Tax System | Annual Earnings | Tax Burden | Net Retained |
|------------|----------------|------------|--------------|
| 30% income tax (typical) | USD 30,000 | USD 9,000 | USD 21,000 |
| 45% income tax (high-rate) | USD 30,000 | USD 13,500 | USD 16,500 |
| GX velocity mechanism (max 6%) | USD 30,000 equivalent | Max USD 1,800 | USD 28,200 |

*The velocity mechanism applies only to savings held above GX 100 for more than 360 accumulated days. It does not apply to income earned and spent within the year. A participant who uses GX actively — spending and receiving regularly — pays no velocity tax.*

**Social media version**: "In most countries, you lose 30-45% of your income to tax before you see it. In GX, you keep 100% of every payment. The only cost: 3-6% on idle savings above a threshold — and only after all your expenses are met."
