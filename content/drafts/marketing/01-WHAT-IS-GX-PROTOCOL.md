## The One-Paragraph Explanation

GX Protocol is a non-profit, public-utility protocol that defines a new kind of money: a fixed-supply, globally unified unit of account that is distributed by grant to every verified participant on Earth. No purchase required. No bank account needed. No inflation by design. No interest on lending. Near-zero cross-border transaction costs. One identity, one account, accessible anywhere.

## How It Works — Simple Version

1. **You verify your identity** — biometric KYC, one-time process
2. **You receive GX units** — a grant based on your registration phase, at no cost
3. **You use them** — buy goods, send money, save, invest in businesses
4. **Cross-border is the same as local** — one GX unit in Kenya is the same GX unit in the Philippines
5. **Your identity is private** — known to the protocol, never disclosed to governments, companies, or third parties

## What Makes GX Different From Everything Else?

### It's not like fiat currency
Fiat currencies are controlled by central banks that can print unlimited amounts. The US dollar has lost 87% of its purchasing power since 1971. GX has a fixed supply — no one can print more.

### It's not like Bitcoin
Bitcoin must be purchased or mined. It's volatile, slow (10-minute blocks), and expensive during congestion. If you lose your private key, your funds are gone permanently. An estimated 15%+ of all mined Bitcoin is already lost forever. GX is distributed by grant, settles in sub-seconds, and can be recovered through identity verification.

### It's not like stablecoins
Stablecoins (USDT, USDC) are digital versions of fiat — they inherit all of fiat's problems (inflation, political control, issuer risk). GX is independent of any fiat currency.

### It's not like CBDCs
Central Bank Digital Currencies give governments full visibility into your transactions. They cost governments USD 50-500M+ to develop. GX provides governments with treasury allocations and compliance infrastructure at zero development cost, while keeping participant identity private.

## Frequently Asked First Questions

**"Is this free? What's the catch?"**
GX units are distributed by grant upon identity verification. There is no purchase. There is no financial risk. The protocol is operated by a non-profit Swiss Foundation. The "catch" is that GX's value depends on participants using it — it is a medium of exchange, not a gift with guaranteed purchasing power.

**"Who pays for this?"**
No one. GX units are created at genesis with a fixed supply. They are not purchased from GX Protocol — they are allocated by protocol rules. The infrastructure is funded by transaction fees (0.05-0.40%) and velocity mechanism collections.

**"Is this a cryptocurrency?"**
GX is a digital medium of exchange recorded on a distributed ledger. Unlike most cryptocurrencies, it requires full identity verification, has built-in compliance (KYC/KYR/AML), and is operated by a non-profit foundation — not a speculative token.
