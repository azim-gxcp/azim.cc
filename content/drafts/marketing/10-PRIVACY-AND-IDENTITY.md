## The Design Principle

GX Protocol knows who you are. No one else does.

Your identity is verified at registration (KYC with biometric confirmation). This identity is known to the protocol. It is **never disclosed to any external party** — not to governments, not to courts, not to partners, not to advertisers, not to anyone.

---

## How Court Orders Work

When a court issues an instruction — freeze an account, distribute an inheritance, block a transaction — the process is strictly one-directional:

1. The court provides identifying details to GX Protocol ("freeze the account of person X at address Y with document number Z")
2. GX Protocol executes the instruction
3. GX Protocol **never discloses identity back** to the court or any external party

The court already knows who the person is — that's how they issued the instruction. GX confirms the match and acts. The flow never reverses.

This prevents governments from using court orders as a channel to extract participant identity data at scale.

## How GX Compares on Privacy

| System | Who Knows Your Identity | Who Can See Your Transactions | Can the Government Access Your Data? |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Cash** | Nobody | Nobody | No (but limited to physical proximity) |
| **Bank account** | Your bank, credit bureaus, data brokers | Your bank, payment processors | Yes — legal requests, tax authorities |
| **Bitcoin** | Nobody (in theory) | Everyone (blockchain is public) | Yes — exchanges link identity to holdings; governments have confiscated Bitcoin |
| **Stablecoins** | Exchange (if KYC'd) | Public blockchain | Yes — USDC has frozen addresses under OFAC instruction |
| **CBDCs** | Your government | Your government | Yes — designed for this purpose |
| **GX Protocol** | The protocol only | The protocol only | No — court orders executed without identity disclosure |

## Bitcoin's Privacy Is Largely Illusory

Bitcoin is pseudonymous on-chain. But:

- The moment Bitcoin is deposited on a regulated exchange, the participant's identity is linked to their holdings
- Governments have routinely compelled exchanges to disclose this information
- Governments have confiscated Bitcoin using exchange-linked identity data
- The only way to preserve Bitcoin's pseudonymity is to never use a regulated exchange — which is impractical for everyday economic life

For the 4 billion participants GX is designed to serve, this is not a viable approach.

## No Data Monetisation

GX Protocol does not gather behavioural data, conduct analysis on participant activity, or use participant data for any commercial purpose. This is not merely because GX is structured as a non-profit — the reason is more fundamental.

GX Protocol issues the monetary unit. An issuer that then seeks to extract value back from its participants — through behavioural analysis, targeted promotion, data sales, or any other commercial mechanism — is in direct conflict with its own purpose. The protocol that creates money has no reason to profit from the people who use it.

Unlike commercial platforms, participants who interact with GX are not the product. They are the reason the protocol exists.

## Account Recovery and Inheritance

### Recovery
An estimated 3-4 million Bitcoin have been permanently lost due to lost private keys. In GX, your identity is your recovery key. A participant who loses their device recovers their account through identity re-verification. Your balance represents your life's work — losing a key should not mean losing everything.

### Inheritance
Through KYR (Know Your Relationship) verification, GX maps verified heir relationships at the protocol level. When a participant dies, their balance is distributed to verified heirs through a formal protocol-level inheritance process. No manual key transfer required. No dependency on the holder having shared credentials before death.

An estimated 20% of all Bitcoin — approximately USD 300 billion — may be permanently inaccessible due to lost keys, many from deaths where keys were not passed on. GX's inheritance mechanism structurally prevents this category of permanent wealth destruction.
