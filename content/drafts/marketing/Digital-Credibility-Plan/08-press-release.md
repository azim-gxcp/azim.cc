# Non-Profit Digital Currency Protocol to Distribute Free Currency to First Billion Participants — With No Exchange Listing, No ICO, and No Token Sale

**Zug, Switzerland** — The GXC Protocol Foundation, a non-profit organization registered in Zug, Switzerland, today announced that its sovereign digital currency protocol, GX Coin, is approaching public activation in Q2/Q3 2026.

Unlike the thousands of cryptocurrency tokens that rely on presales, exchange listings, and speculative trading, GX Coin will be distributed for free to individuals through a "Genesis Distribution" — allocating units directly to registered participants based on a tiered system that rewards early adoption.

The protocol features:
- **1.25 trillion total supply**, permanently fixed and non-inflationary
- **Gold-anchored reference value** (1 GX = 1 gram of gold at genesis)
- **0% interest rate**, with capital provided through profit-sharing
- **Demurrage-based circulation incentive** on large idle balances
- **No exchange listing** — by design, not by limitation

"We're not building a speculative asset. We're building economic infrastructure," said M Azim Abdul Majeed, Lead Architect of GX Coin Protocol. "The crypto industry has created enormous wealth for a small number of early investors while doing almost nothing for the billions of people who are underserved by the global financial system. GX Coin is designed to correct that."

### How It Works

GX Coin's Genesis Distribution allocates units to individuals proportionally across nations based on population. The first 100 million registrants receive GX 500 each. Government treasuries, charitable pools, and business lending facilities are funded simultaneously — creating a complete economy at activation, not just a token.

The protocol runs on Proof of Authority consensus with professional validators, and its core monetary policies — including the fixed supply and interest prohibition — are immutably embedded in the blockchain and cannot be changed by any governance body.

### Background

GX Coin Protocol has been in development since October 2023, originally under the name "Goodness Exchange." The project's protocol specification, now in its eighth major revision (v8.0), presents an original monetary design built from first principles. The resulting model — featuring circulation incentives on idle capital and profit-sharing instead of interest — independently parallels concepts tested historically by the Swiss WIR Bank (1934-present) and other complementary currency experiments.

The GXC Protocol Foundation is a registered non-profit in Zug, Switzerland, with no shareholders. Commercial operations are managed through GXC Protocol PTE LTD, registered in Singapore.
