## We Charge Interest on Idleness, Not on Borrowing

| | Traditional Finance | Typical DeFi | GX Coin Protocol |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Borrowing cost** | Interest (often compound) | Interest + protocol fees | 0% — capital via profit-sharing |
| **Saving incentive** | Interest earned on deposits | Yield farming / staking rewards | Modest savings protected; hoarding taxed (demurrage) |
| **Who bears the risk of failure** | Borrower (debt remains) | Borrower (liquidation) | Shared — investor and entrepreneur together |

**What this means:** If your business fails, you don't carry a debt burden. If it succeeds, your investor shares in the profit. Risk is aligned, not offloaded.

## Our Consensus Is Managed, Not Anarchic

We use **Proof of Authority** — professional validator nodes operated by vetted institutions with financial deposits and performance requirements.

*"But isn't that centralized?"*

Yes, partially. And intentionally. Here's why:

A monetary system requires stability, predictability, and accountability. Proof of Work (Bitcoin) wastes enormous energy. Proof of Stake concentrates power among the wealthy. Both allow anonymous participants to influence the network.

Proof of Authority means every validator is known, accountable, and removable. They're compensated from transaction fees, not from token inflation. And the core economic rules (supply, interest prohibition) are immutable — no validator, and no governance body, can change them.

The blockchain is permissioned. The monetary policy is not.
