## The Dynamic Approach

### Why Not Fixed-in-Time

Events are unfolding by the hour. Tariff announcements, CBDC developments, central bank decisions, military operations, legislative changes, market moves — each one is a window into the structural mechanics this series examines.

A fixed calendar becomes outdated within days. Instead, this calendar provides:

1. **A bank of 30+ post concepts** organised by theme
2. **A current-events trigger list** — what headlines to watch for and how to respond
3. **A weekly rhythm** — alternating between structural analysis and current-event response
4. **A consistent voice** — every post follows the same structure regardless of topic

## Post Structure Template

Every post, regardless of topic, follows this structure:

```
[HOOK — 1-2 sentences. A number, a fact, or a question that stops the scroll.]

[CONTEXT — 3-4 sentences. Where does this number come from? What is the mechanism?]

[STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS — 3-4 sentences. WHY does this exist? Not who is to blame —
but what incentive structure produces this outcome.]

[THE DESIGN QUESTION — 1-2 sentences. "What if this were designed differently?"
Do not answer it. Let the reader sit with it.]

[SOURCE — Cite the data. Every number traceable.]
```

**Length**: 1,200-2,000 characters for LinkedIn. Shorter for X/Twitter.

## Rules for Every Post

1. **No product promotion** — never mention specific projects, protocols, or products as solutions
2. **Acknowledge sources** — Dixon, McCormack, and any other commentator whose analysis you reference
3. **Cite data** — every quantitative claim must have a named source
4. **Structural, not conspiratorial** — describe mechanisms, not villains
5. **Questions, not answers** — end with a design question, not a pitch
6. **Fluid and current** — adapt to what happened today, this week. The post bank provides the structural analysis; the current event provides the entry point
7. **Never religious** — first-principles analysis only. No theological framing.
8. **Never adversarial** — do not attack institutions, governments, or individuals by name (except when citing their own published statements)
9. **Personal voice** — this is AAM's perspective, not an institutional voice
10. **Invite scrutiny** — "If I have this wrong, I want to know" is stronger than certainty
