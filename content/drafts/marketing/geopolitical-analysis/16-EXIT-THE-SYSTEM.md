## The Argument: Every Investment in the System Is Complicity

### Simon Dixon's Position

Simon Dixon makes a radical argument that most financial commentators avoid:

1. **If you invest in the S&P 500, you fund the Military Industrial Complex** — Lockheed Martin, Raytheon, General Dynamics, Boeing Defense, Palantir, and every military contractor is in the index
2. **If you receive yield from the financial system, you are part of the problem** — yield is generated through the extraction mechanisms of the FIC
3. **If you hold equities in companies complicit in the system, you are complicit** — understanding the system and choosing to remain in it is a moral choice

His exact framing: "If I receive yield then I'm part of the problem... I don't judge anyone that does this... but the Nazis were just doing their job... if you understand it and you know it's evil yet you hold on to those equities, you're complicit."

### What Dixon Did

He fully withdrew:
- Sold all equity positions that involved counterparty risk
- Stopped investing in stocks, bonds, and index funds
- Moved to self-custody Bitcoin and physical gold
- Invests only in community projects — "fish and chip shops, farms, schools"
- Rejects BlackRock managing his assets
- Rejects the S&P 500 as a vehicle

### The Three-Word Summary: "Exit the system."

## Counterparty Risk as Theft of Time

### The Mechanism

Every investment with counterparty risk — meaning any investment where another party's decisions affect your outcome — steals your time:

| Investment Type | Time Theft |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Private equity** | Board meetings, legal disputes, rogue founders, compliance |
| **Public equities** | Monitoring, earnings calls, market volatility, emotional energy |
| **Real estate via funds** | Property management, tenant issues, legal requirements |
| **Angel investing** | "Every single one of them let me down... I got scammed everywhere" |
| **Bonds/lending** | Default risk, legal recovery, counterparty assessment |

### Self-Custody Eliminates Time Theft

| Self-Custody Asset | Time Cost |
|-------------------|-----------|
| **Bitcoin (self-custody)** | Near zero — "Bitcoin in self-custody doesn't give me grief" |
| **Physical gold** | Near zero — "Gold bars don't give me grief" |
| **GX units** | Near zero — protocol-governed, no board meetings, no counterparty |

### The Deeper Point

Time is the only non-renewable resource. Every counterparty relationship converts your time into their problems. Self-sovereign assets — assets you hold without depending on anyone — return your time to you.

**GX connection**: GX units in a participant's wallet have zero counterparty risk. No fund manager. No board. No counterparty. The protocol governs the rules. The participant governs their own holdings.

## Building Communities: How New Industries Form Around a New Medium

### Simon Dixon's Vision

Dixon invests in:
- "Somebody who wants to create a fish and chip shop on the island"
- "Someone who wants to build a farm and provide food to the community"
- "A new school system outside of the government schools"

This is not charity. It is the foundation of a parallel economy — one that operates on real production, real services, and real human needs rather than financial extraction.

### How GX Makes This Structural

Without GX, community economies are limited:
- They still need fiat for transactions (extracting fees to banks and payment processors)
- They still save in fiat (losing purchasing power to inflation)
- They still borrow at interest (transferring wealth to lenders)
- They still pay remittance fees to move money between communities

With GX, community economies become self-reinforcing:

```
Community Business (farm, shop, school)
    ↓ accepts GX
    ↓ pays suppliers in GX
    ↓ pays workers in GX
    ↓ workers spend GX at other community businesses
    ↓ velocity mechanism recirculates idle GX
    ↓ loan pool provides interest-free capital for new businesses
    ↓ community grows, attracting more businesses
    ↓ critical mass → full economic ecosystem
```

### The Community Flywheel

| **Growth** | 50-500 participants, diverse businesses | GX loan pool for business capital |
