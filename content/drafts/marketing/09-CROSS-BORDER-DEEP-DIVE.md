## The Problem

Global remittances totalled approximately USD 860 billion in 2024. The average cost was 6.2% globally, exceeding 8% for Sub-Saharan Africa corridors.

That means approximately **USD 48-53 billion per year** is extracted from the world's most financially vulnerable populations — migrant workers sending money home to their families. These fees represent friction, not value. The intermediaries that capture them produce nothing; they exploit the structural inefficiency of a system that requires currency conversion, correspondent banking chains, and regulatory checkpoints that serve institutional interests, not participant interests.

Every dollar extracted in remittance fees is a dollar taken from a family's food budget, a child's school fees, a small business's working capital.

## Why Traditional Fees Are So High

A typical cross-border wire involves:

1. **Sender's bank** — charges a wire fee (USD 15-45)
2. **Correspondent bank 1** — takes a lifting fee
3. **Correspondent bank 2** — takes another lifting fee
4. **Recipient's bank** — charges an incoming wire fee
5. **Currency conversion** — 0.5-2.0% spread captured by the converting institution
6. **Compliance processing** — each intermediary performs its own AML/KYC checks

Total: USD 20-50+ per wire, plus the hidden exchange rate spread. Settlement takes 2-5 business days.

In GX: one transfer. Sub-second settlement. 0.15-0.25% fee. No intermediaries. No conversion. No spread. The GX unit sent in Nairobi is the same GX unit received in Manila.

## Real-World Comparisons

### SWIFT
- ~68 million messages per day
- ~USD 5 trillion daily in payment instructions
- Total end-to-end cost: USD 20-50+ per transaction
- Settlement: 2-5 business days

### Visa/Mastercard
- ~1.15 billion transactions per day combined
- Merchant pays 2.5-3.0% per transaction
- Visa's annual revenue: USD 35.9 billion
- Mastercard's annual revenue: USD 28.2 billion

### UPI (India) — What's Possible
- ~675 million transactions per day
- Near-zero cost (government-subsidised)
- Proves that high-volume, low-cost digital payments work at national scale

### GX Protocol
- 0.05-0.40% transaction fees
- Sub-second finality
- No conversion required across borders
- No intermediary banks
