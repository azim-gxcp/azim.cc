This dashboard identifies where GX Protocol has the strongest opportunity among Gen Z (ages 15-29) and Gen Alpha (ages 2-14, becoming eligible from 2028). Gen Z is the most digitally connected, economically squeezed, and institutionally skeptical generation in history. They have high mobile/internet penetration but low bank account ownership. They have entrepreneurial energy but limited access to capital. GX Protocol offers them a grant (average GX 210, approximately USD 25,200 at genesis rate) with no capital required, interest-free business lending through profit-sharing, and a digital-native economy to build from scratch.

Gen Z, as the most digitally connected generation, is expected to be the first wave of adopters, disproportionately filling the early, higher-value distribution phases (Phase 1A: GX 1,000, Phase 1B: GX 750, Phase 1C: GX 500). This results in a higher weighted average of GX 210 per Gen Z participant compared to the global average of GX 136.

The Gen Z Impact Index scores each country on Youth Need (60% - financial exclusion, unemployment, economic vulnerability, youth population share) and Youth Readiness (40% - mobile, internet, youth population scale).

Data: World Bank Global Findex 2025 (youth account ownership ages 15-24), ILO/World Bank (youth unemployment), UN Population Division (age demographics), ITU (mobile/internet).

Important Disclaimer

Distribution amounts are illustrative projections. GX units are issued to verified individuals aged 15-70 across six distribution phases. The Gen Z average grant of GX 210 (USD 25,200) reflects this generation's expected concentration in early distribution phases due to higher digital adoption rates; the global population average is GX 136 (USD 16,320). Not all eligible youth may register. Actual amounts depend on individual registration. Youth population 15-29 is estimated at 1.5x the verified 15-24 cohort from UN data.
