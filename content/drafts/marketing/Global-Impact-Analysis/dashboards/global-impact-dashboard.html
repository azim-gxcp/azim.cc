GX Protocol -Global Impact Analysis

Data-Driven Assessment of Where GX Creates the Most Value
201 Countries  |  World Bank, IMF, ITU Data

This dashboard presents a data-driven assessment of where GX Coin Protocol -a non-profit, fixed-supply digital currency -creates the most value across 195 sovereign nations. The analysis combines financial exclusion data, macroeconomic indicators, and digital infrastructure metrics to produce a GX Impact Index that scores each country on two axes: how much the country needs what GX offers (Need), and whether the population can adopt a digital currency (Readiness).

GX Coin Protocol distributes currency directly to individuals as grants -no purchase required. Governments receive treasury allocations of an average of GX 60 per participant for government treasury (approximately USD 7,200 per participant) and an average of GX 136 per participant for individuals (approximately USD 16,320 per participant). These averages account for six distribution phases and eligibility limited to ages 15-70 (approximately 60% of total population). All values use the genesis calibration of 1 GX = 1 gram of gold = USD 120. The protocol features a fixed supply of 1.25 trillion GX units, 0% interest on all lending (profit-sharing only), and a velocity mechanism (3-6% annual on large idle balances) that funds government treasuries, charitable causes, and universal basic income.

Data sources: World Bank Global Findex 2025 (account ownership), World Bank WDI (GDP, population), IMF World Economic Outlook (inflation), ITU (mobile and internet penetration), World Bank KNOMAD (remittances), World Bank IDS (external debt). All data cross-verified against independent sources. See the methodology document for full details.

The GX Impact Index is a composite score (0-100) combining two weighted dimensions:

Need Score (60% weight)

Measures how much a country needs what GX offers:

  • Unbanked population % (15%) -adults without financial accounts
  • Remittance dependency (12%) -remittances received as % of GDP
  • Economic vulnerability (10%) -inverse of GDP per capita
  • Currency instability (13%) -consumer price inflation rate
  • Fiscal stress (10%) -external debt as % of GNI

Readiness Score (40% weight)

Measures whether the population can adopt a digital currency:

  • Mobile phone penetration (15%) -subscriptions per 100 people
  • Internet access (10%) -% of population using internet
  • Population scale (5%) -larger populations create larger ecosystems
  • Relative grant impact (5%) -inverse of GDP per capita (grant means more in poorer countries)
Tier Classification: Tier 2 (60-74): Strong Impact  |  Tier 3 (45-59): Moderate Impact  |  Tier 4 (30-44): Strategic Impact  |  Tier 5 (0-29): Adoption-Led

Countries with high Need but low Readiness cannot adopt. Countries with high Readiness but low Need lack motivation. The sweet spot is high Need + high Readiness.

Disclaimer: The treasury and individual allocation amounts shown are illustrative projections based on estimated eligible population (60% of total, approximating ages 15-70), not guaranteed outcomes. Treasury allocations use an average of GX 60 per participant (50% at GX 80 Phase 1, 50% at GX 40 Phase 2). Individual allocations use an average of GX 136 per participant (weighted across six distribution phases targeting 4 billion participants). Actual distribution amounts for each country will differ because: (1) GX units are issued only to verified individuals aged 15-70, not the entire population; (2) distribution occurs across six phases with declining per-participant grants; (3) allocation within each phase is proportional to registered participants, not total citizens; (4) the protocol does not control registration - if not all eligible citizens within a country register, that country's total allocation will be lower. These figures represent estimated capacity at the genesis reference rate of 1 GX = USD 120, not predetermined outcomes.
