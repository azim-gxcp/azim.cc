## The Mathematical Problem with Interest

In any closed monetary system where the money supply = M, if loans are issued at interest, total debt = M + interest. This means total claims on money exceed total money in existence.

This is not theory — it is arithmetic. The result is that debt-based systems can only function by continuously creating new money (inflation) or by accepting widespread defaults. Global debt-to-GDP reached approximately 350% by 2025.

GX sidesteps this entirely: total claims on money can never exceed total money. The supply is fixed at 1.25 trillion GX units.

## How GX Lending Works

1. GX Protocol provides capital to FSP partners on a profit-sharing basis
2. FSPs lend to businesses and individuals — at terms they set, but always profit-sharing (no interest)
3. If the business succeeds: profit is shared between the business, the FSP, and the protocol
4. If the business fails: 50% forgiveness after 10 years of genuine inability; 2 guarantors per loan
5. Single-identity enforcement means businesses cannot take a loan and disappear

Interest-free, profit-sharing finance is not theoretical. It represents a **USD 4+ trillion industry** operating across 80+ countries through cooperative banks, credit unions, microfinance cooperatives, and profit-sharing financial institutions.

GX adds what traditional institutions lack:

- **Automatic enforcement** — profit-sharing payments collected at protocol level
- **Identity-verified participants** — single account, single identity, impossible to hide activity
- **Guarantor mechanism** — 2 KYR-verified guarantors per loan
