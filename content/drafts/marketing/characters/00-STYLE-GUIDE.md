# GX Protocol — Illustration Style Guide

**This document is binding. Every character illustration and every animated frame follows these rules.**

---

## 1. Overall Aesthetic

- **Style family**: Soft cartoon / rounded vector.
- **Reference feel**: Duolingo, Headspace, Lottie explainer animations, modern fintech onboarding illustrations.
- **NOT**: realistic, photorealistic, anime, semi-realistic, 3D-rendered, pixel art, hand-sketched.
- **Mood**: warm, approachable, optimistic, educational — never corporate, never childish, never hype.

---

## 2. Proportions

- **Head-to-body ratio**: ~5 heads tall (stylized but adult — not chibi, not realistic-8-heads).
- **Head**: slightly larger than realistic, emphasizing face and expression.
- **Hands**: simplified — soft mittens or 4-finger hands with soft edges.
- **Feet**: simplified shapes, no detailed shoe anatomy.
- **No exaggerated sexualization** — bodies are simplified, modest, functional.

---

## 3. Line Work

- **No harsh black outlines.** Instead: soft colored outlines 1–2 shades darker than the fill, OR fully line-less with clean shape separation.
- **Line weight** (if used): consistent, medium-thin.
- **Inner details**: minimal — suggest folds/creases with 1–2 subtle lines, not many.

---

### Shading

- Flat color with ONE soft shadow layer (10–15% darker).
- Occasional highlight (5% lighter) on rounded surfaces.
- **No gradients on skin or clothing** (except optional soft cheek blush).
- Background can use a soft radial or linear gradient.

## 6. Face System

All characters share a simplified face grammar so expressions remain consistent across the cast.

- **Eyes**: either (a) large oval with visible iris + small highlight, OR (b) closed-curve "smile eyes" for joyful moments.
- **Eyebrows**: thin, expressive — a primary vehicle for emotion.
- **Nose**: small soft curve or dot — never detailed nostrils.
- **Mouth**: simplified — open smile, closed smile, neutral line, concerned frown. No detailed teeth.
- **Cheeks**: optional soft pink blush circle for warmth.
- **Ears**: simple rounded shapes.

**Expression defaults** (each character's resting face):
- Curious (raised brow, slight smile, wide eyes)
- Warm (soft smile, relaxed brows)
- Focused (steady eyes, neutral mouth)
- Skeptical (one brow raised, tight mouth)
- Determined (firm brows, slight set jaw)
- Playful (smile eyes, open grin)

---

## 7. Hair

- **Bold silhouettes** — hair is a primary recognition cue.
- **Max 2 tones**: base color + optional darker or lighter strands.
- **No individual strand rendering** — hair is a clean shape with suggested flow.
- **Avoid culturally specific styles** (no religious head coverings, no tribal-specific markers). Aim for universal haircuts: bob, buzz, curls, braids, ponytail, bun, short crop, shoulder-length, textured crop, afro puff, undercut, etc. — distributed across the cast.

---

## 8. Clothing Rules

- **Job-readable silhouettes** — a nurse reads as a nurse from 30 meters.
- **Functional simplicity** — no intricate patterns, no jewelry clutter.
- **Signature garment** — each character owns one hero garment (jacket, apron, vest, tunic) that never changes.
- **Modern, timeless, global** — no fad-specific trends, no deeply culturally specific dress.
- **No religious clothing or iconography.**

### 8a. Modesty Standard (Binding)

Every character across every tier is **modestly dressed**. This is not a stylistic preference — it is a rule.

- **Coverage**: shoulders, chest, midriff, back, and upper legs are always covered.
- **Tops**: long-sleeve or short-sleeve with a crew or round neckline. No deep V-necks, no spaghetti straps, no halter tops, no crop tops, no off-shoulder cuts, no low necklines, no bare midriffs, no backless garments.
- **Tank tops** are allowed only as an under-layer beneath a jacket, cardigan, or coat — never as an outer garment on their own.
- **Bottoms**: full-length trousers, full-length jeans, chinos, work pants, or loose joggers are the default across the roster. Skirts and dresses, when used, are **knee-length or longer** — never pencil-tight or above the knee. No shorts above the knee.
- **Fit**: relaxed, comfortable, functional. **No tight, body-hugging, or form-revealing cuts** on tops or bottoms. Clothing drapes and suggests the body — it does not outline it.
- **Workwear exceptions**: athletic instructors wear long-sleeve athletic tops + loose joggers (never cropped, never tight). Medical staff wear standard scrubs or lab coats over full-coverage clothing.
- **No swimwear scenes, no beachwear scenes** in any video.
- **Jewelry**: minimal — simple earrings, a pendant, a watch, a bracelet, a ring. Never gaudy, never conspicuous.

If a proposed scene or garment risks failing this standard, revise the scene. The cast's professionalism and accessibility is part of the GX Protocol's universal appeal.

---

## 9. Props & Accessories

- Each character carries 1–2 job-identifying props (e.g., teacher's book, farmer's crate, carpenter's saw).
- Props are stylized and simplified — not photo-real.
- GX app / phone / wallet visual is standardized (see section 11).

---

## 10. Environment & Background

- **Minimal**: soft color fields, suggested horizon lines, a few shape-based scene props.
- **Never photographic**.
- **Scenes**: market, farm, classroom, clinic, workshop, café, home, riverside, office, street — rendered as simple planes with a few iconic props.
- **No text on background signage** unless the story requires it (then: stylized, readable, short).

---

## 11. GX Visual Motifs

Consistency across every video:

- **GX coin icon**: a soft gold circle with a stylized "GX" monogram. Not shiny, not metallic-rendered — flat gold.
- **GX wallet**: phone mockup with a clean interface — soft gold number + warm background.
- **Grant moment**: a soft glow or gentle particle burst when grants arrive — never flashy fireworks.
- **Velocity motif**: flowing curved arrows suggesting circulation (not aggressive lines).
- **Pool motif** (loan pool, treasury): a calm pond or bowl shape, suggesting collective gathering — not a vault, not a safe.

---

## 12. Do-Not List

- ❌ Realistic rendering of any kind
- ❌ Harsh black outlines
- ❌ Detailed textures (skin pores, fabric weave, hair strands)
- ❌ Religious symbols, markers, or attire
- ❌ Currency symbols of other currencies as primary visuals (USD, EUR, etc. — only when the story needs them, always as stylized shapes)
- ❌ Gold bars, stacks of cash, or "wealth" clichés
- ❌ Vaults, safes, or "locked money" imagery
- ❌ Rocket ships, "to the moon", or crypto-bro visuals
- ❌ Charts with exploding upward arrows
- ❌ Lambos, yachts, luxury markers
- ❌ Fear imagery (crashes, downfalls, broken lines) unless specifically illustrating a critique of the old system, and even then: soft, not alarmist

---

## 13. Animation Considerations

When these characters are animated:
- **Rigging**: simple bone rigs — head, torso, arms, legs, hair bounce.
- **Movement**: smooth, intentional, unhurried. No jittery or frantic motion.
- **Gestures**: expressive hands (pointing, open palm, thumbs up) used generously.
- **Walk cycles**: simple 4-8 frame cycles, stylized.
- **Held objects**: props are parented to hands cleanly, no floating.

---

## 14. Consistency Audit

Before any character illustration is approved for production:

- [ ] Silhouette is unique and recognizable
- [ ] Signature colors are present
- [ ] Job-identifying prop is visible
- [ ] Skin tone matches the canonical reference
- [ ] Hair style matches the canonical reference
- [ ] Expression default is visible in neutral pose
- [ ] No forbidden elements (section 12) present
- [ ] Proportions match the 5-head standard
- [ ] Modesty standard (section 8a) met — coverage, fit, and length all compliant
