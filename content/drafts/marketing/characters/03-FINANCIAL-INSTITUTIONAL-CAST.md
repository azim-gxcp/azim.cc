# Tier 3 — Financial & Institutional Cast (8 Characters)

These characters represent the **traditional monetary world**. They exist so that GX can be explained by contrast — how venture capital works vs. how the Business Loan Pool works; how a banker prices a loan vs. how the protocol prices one; how a trader speculates vs. how GX resists speculation.

**These characters are not villains.** They are the real-world counterparts the audience already knows. Their world is legitimate. The stories show *how GX complements or differs from* their world — not how GX replaces it.

When these characters speak in their professional voice, they use traditional-finance language (invest, return, portfolio, yield). This is intentional — it is the educational contrast. GX-side characters answer in protocol-compliant language.

## 36. MAX — Commercial Banker (34)

- **Silhouette**: Tall, upright, suited, briefcase, behind a desk or at a branch counter.
- **Skin tone**: Peach
- **Hair**: Dark blonde, slicked back, short on the sides, clean professional.
- **Face**: Square jaw, blue-gray eyes, clean-shaven, a measured smile.
- **Body**: Athletic-average, trained to look confident.
- **Signature outfit**: Charcoal suit, white shirt, indigo tie, polished black shoes, a slim wristwatch.
- **Signature colors**: Charcoal, Indigo, Paper
- **Props**: Laptop, brochures, a bank-branded pen, loan documents, a coffee cup.
- **Personality**: Polished, numbers-driven, explains interest rates with a practiced smile, genuinely helpful within the system he works in.
- **Story role**: **Traditional banking contrast**. When a character needs a loan, Max explains the bank's terms: collateral, credit score, interest rate. The story then shows the Business Loan Pool alternative. Max is curious about GX and asks good questions — he represents the institutional observer, not the adversary.
