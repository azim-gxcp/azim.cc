# GX Protocol — Character Universe

**Purpose**: A consistent cast of illustrated characters for educational videos explaining how the GX Coin Protocol functions in the real world — covering adoption, grants, velocity, purchasing power, lending, cross-border flows, and the interaction between GX and the traditional monetary world.

**Visual Approach**: Soft cartoon, rounded shapes, non-realistic. Friendly, universal, approachable — in the spirit of Duolingo, Headspace, and Lottie-style explainer illustrations.

**Age Range**: 15–35 across the full roster.

**Cast Size**: 48 characters, organized into 4 tiers.

## How the Tiers Work

**Tier 1 — Main Cast (10)**
The recurring faces. The audience grows attached to them. Every video should feature at least one. They carry the continuity of the GX story across episodes.

**Tier 2 — Supporting Cast (24)**
Scenario-specific. A story about rural food needs Arun and Finn; a story about cross-border remittance needs Tessa. They appear when the scenario requires their role.

**Tier 3 — Financial/Institutional Cast (8)**
The traditional monetary world. They exist so GX can be explained *by contrast* — how a VC funds a startup vs. how a Business Loan Pool does; how a banker charges interest vs. how GX doesn't. They are not villains — they are the real-world counterparts the audience already knows.

**Tier 4 — Protocol Cast (6)**
People wearing "official" GX roles. They represent the mechanisms of the protocol itself — the validator, the KYC reviewer, the partner onboarder, the treasury steward.

## Design Principles

1. **Silhouette first** — each character must be recognizable by outline alone. Hair shape and body silhouette do the heavy lifting.
2. **Signature color** — every character has 2–3 anchor colors that appear in every scene they are in.
3. **Job-identifying prop** — a tool, garment, or accessory makes the role instantly readable.
4. **Universal features** — deliberately abstract ethnicity. Skin tone, hair, and features are mixed across the roster without mapping to any single region.
5. **No religious or overtly cultural markers** — no headscarves, turbans, crosses, tilaks, etc. The protocol serves all; the cast reflects that.
6. **Expression default** — every character has a "resting" expression that reflects personality (curious, warm, skeptical, determined, etc.).

---

## Language Discipline (per GX Protocol Language Specification)

- Characters are **participants** in the protocol, never "users" or "customers".
- GX amounts: **GX 500** (never "500 GX" or "500 coins").
- 1 GX = 1 gram of gold = USD 120 (use this conversion when showing equivalents).
- No speculative language ("invest in GX", "GX will rise", "returns"). Traditional-finance characters may use that language when representing *their* world — this is the educational contrast.

## Usage

Each character's entry is structured the same way, so story writers and illustrators can pull exactly what they need:

```
NAME — Role (Age)
├── Silhouette
├── Skin tone
├── Hair
├── Face
├── Body
├── Signature outfit
├── Signature colors
├── Props / accessories
├── Personality
└── Story role in the GX universe
```

Treat these entries as the canonical reference. Once a character is illustrated once, the reference should never drift — across every video, Maya the teacher has the same hair, the same coat, the same warm yellow.
