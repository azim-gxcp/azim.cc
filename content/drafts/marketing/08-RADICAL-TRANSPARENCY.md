## The Strategy

No government has ever published an honest analysis of what is wrong with its currency. No central bank has ever voluntarily listed the weaknesses of its monetary policy. Bitcoin's protocol specification does not mention its failure as a medium of exchange.

GX Protocol publishes all of this, intentionally, because a system confident enough to show its weaknesses in public is a system that has answered those weaknesses.

## What GX Publishes

- The complete economic model
- The distribution schedule
- The protocol-defined constraints framework
- The fee structure
- The soundness analysis — including its criticisms and the score below 100%
- Every identified weakness alongside its specific mitigation

## The Invitation

GX invites:
- **Economists** to challenge the velocity mechanism's counter-cyclical claims
- **Monetary policy researchers** to stress-test the fixed-supply model
- **Financial regulators** to examine the compliance architecture
- **Cryptographers** to evaluate the post-quantum migration roadmap
- **Development economists** to assess the grant-based distribution model

**What GX does with feedback**:
- Publishes all credible critiques alongside responses
- Updates the soundness score if warranted
- Incorporates valid concerns into protocol design

**What GX does not do**:
- Dismiss criticism
- Respond with promotional language
- Claim that critics "don't understand"

## Six Criteria for Sound Money

| Criterion | GX Assessment |
|-----------|--------------|
| Preserves purchasing power | Fixed supply — zero protocol-level inflation |
| Accessible without prior wealth | Grant-based — requires only verified identity |
| Usable for daily transactions | Sub-second finality, 0.05-0.40% fees |
| Resistant to manipulation | No one can alter the supply; velocity rates adjustable only by supermajority |
| Governable without centralisation | 17-member board, transitioning to participant-validator governance |
| Survivable beyond its creators | Swiss Foundation, succession planning, protocol defaults |
