## GX vs Bitcoin — Detailed

### Where Bitcoin Succeeds
Bitcoin proved that digital scarcity works. The concept of a mathematically enforced fixed supply — not subject to any government's printing press — is Bitcoin's permanent contribution to monetary history. It has 16+ years of proven operation.

### Where GX Goes Further

| Dimension | Bitcoin | GX Protocol |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Distribution | Must be purchased or mined | Distributed by grant — no capital required |
| Transaction cost | USD 1-50+ during congestion | 0.05-0.40% of transaction value |
| Transaction speed | 10-minute blocks | Sub-second finality |
| Account recovery | Lost key = permanent loss. An estimated 15%+ of all mined Bitcoin is already lost beyond recovery | Identity-based recovery |
| Inheritance | No mechanism. An estimated 20% of all Bitcoin (approximately USD 300B) may be permanently inaccessible | Formal protocol-level inheritance via KYR |
| Privacy (in practice) | Pseudonymous on-chain, but identity linked at any regulated exchange. Governments have compelled exchanges to disclose holdings and confiscated Bitcoin | Identity known to protocol; never disclosed to any external party |
| Medium of exchange | Low — volatility and hoarding incentives make daily use impractical | Designed specifically for daily transactions |
| Anti-hoarding | None — rational behaviour is to hold and wait | Velocity mechanism converts holding incentive into productive-use incentive |
| Lending | No built-in protocol | Interest-free at protocol level |
| Energy | ~100 TWh/year (Proof-of-Work) | Negligible (QBFT consensus) |

### On Bitcoin's "Intrinsic Value"
Bitcoin proponents assert that its value derives from the energy expended to mine it. If so, it would be priced in kilowatt-hours, not in US dollars. The fact that Bitcoin is universally denominated and traded in fiat currencies reveals that its market price is a function of speculative demand, not intrinsic utility. Its price behaviour — extreme volatility, 70-80% drawdowns, speculative cycles — is characteristic of a speculative asset, not a medium of exchange.

## GX vs CBDCs — Detailed

### The Core Difference
CBDCs are the existing fiat system on digital rails. They inherit all of fiat's properties: inflation risk, income visibility, national borders, and political control of monetary policy. GX is a new kind of money.

| Dimension | CBDCs | GX Protocol |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| Income visibility | Full government transaction visibility; income taxable at source | No government access to participant transaction data |
| Inflation | Central bank controls supply; inflationary risk is structural | Fixed supply — zero inflation by design |
| Cross-border | Each CBDC is national; international use requires conversion | Single global unit; no conversion required |
| Privacy | Government has full surveillance capability | Identity never disclosed externally |
| Cost to government | USD 50M-500M+ per jurisdiction; 3-7 years deployment | Zero development cost; 4-6 month onboarding |
| Distribution | Through commercial banks; requires existing financial infrastructure | Direct grant to every verified participant; no bank account required |
| Surveillance | High — by design | Low — by design |

### What Governments Get from GX (Without Building a CBDC)
- Automatic treasury allocation proportional to population
- 40% of velocity mechanism revenue from their jurisdiction's participants
- KYC-verified financial inclusion infrastructure
- Zero development cost, zero collection infrastructure
- All compliance benefits of a CBDC without the surveillance trade-off

## GX vs Stablecoins — Detailed

### The Core Difference
Stablecoins solved one problem: they made digital dollars. That is useful, but it is not new money — it is existing money in a new container.

| Dimension | USDT / USDC | GX Protocol |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Value backing | Fiat reserves (subject to audit risk) | Fixed supply; no fiat dependency |
| Issuer risk | Private companies (Tether Ltd., Circle) | Swiss Foundation — non-commercial |
| Fiat dependency | Inherits all USD monetary policy and inflation | Independent of any fiat currency |
| Sanctions exposure | USDC has frozen addresses under OFAC instruction | No unilateral freeze by any external party |
| Distribution | Must be purchased; requires existing fiat | Distributed by grant; no purchase required |
| Inflation protection | None — tracks USD, which loses purchasing power | Full — fixed supply |

## GX vs SDR (Special Drawing Rights)

The SDR is the closest existing analogue to what GX aspires to be — a supra-national monetary unit. But the SDR was designed for governments, not people.

| Dimension | IMF SDR | GX Protocol |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Accessible to | IMF member governments only | Every verified individual participant globally |
| Transaction use | Settlement between central banks; no retail or commercial use | Designed for retail, commercial, remittance, and institutional use |
| Allocation | Proportional to IMF quota (wealthy nations receive most) | Proportional to population |
| Governance | US has effective veto | No single country has veto |
| Speed | Days to weeks | Sub-second |
